dissipate

___

Verb

/ˈdɪsɪpeɪt/

__

1 (with reference to a feeling or emotion) disappear or cause to disappear

    he wanted to dissipate his anger
    the concern she'd felt for him had wholly dissipated

1.1 disperse or scatter

    the cloud of smoke dissipated

2 waste or fritter away (money, energy, or resources)

    he inherited, but then dissipated, his father's fortune

2.1 cause (energy) to be lost through its conversion to heat

    no power is dissipated in this sort of control element
