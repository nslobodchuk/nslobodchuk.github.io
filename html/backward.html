backward

___

Adjective

/ˈbakwəd/

__

1 directed behind or to the rear

    she left the room without a backward glance

1.1 reverting to an inferior state; retrograde

    the decision was a backward step

2 having made less progress than is normal or expected

    a backward agricultural country

2.1 (of a person) having learning difficulties

    a lively child but a bit backward

3 lacking the confidence to do (something)

    he was not backward in displaying his talents

4 (of a fielding position) behind an imaginary line passing through the stumps at the batsman's end at right angles to the wicket

    backward square leg
