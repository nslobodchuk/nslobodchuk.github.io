rout

___

Noun

/raʊt/

__

1 a disorderly retreat of defeated troops

    the retreat degenerated into a rout

1.1 a decisive defeat

    the party lost more than half their seats in the rout

2 an assembly of people who have made a move towards committing an illegal act which would constitute an offence of riot.

2.1 a disorderly or tumultuous crowd of people

    a rout of strangers ought not to be admitted

3 a large evening party or reception.

4 a pack of wolves

    a rout of wolves consumed the last of the carcass

___

Verb

/raʊt/

__

1 defeat and cause to retreat in disorder

    in a matter of minutes the attackers were routed

__

1 cut a groove, or any pattern not extending to the edges, in (a wooden or metal surface)

    you routed each plank all along its length

2 (of an animal) turn up ground with its snout in search of food.

2.1 rummage about.

3 find or retrieve

    as I routed out the lantern, the telephone jangled

3.1 force (someone) from a place

    Simon routed him from the stables
